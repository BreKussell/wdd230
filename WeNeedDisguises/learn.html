Useful Things!

Need Help? Feel free to ask us a Question.

Tool Why
For Foam, 3D Prints and Worbla
Box Cutter A box cutter is going to be your best friend when working with foams. It makes getting around curves and tight places easier than trying to use scissors
Dremel A Dremel or any other rotary tool is going to be used for carving and sculpting of your foam.
Sandpaper/Sanding sponge Sandpaper is very useful. You can technically use it for carving foam it's just not recommended. What sandpaper is most useful for is for pulling of finishes from things such as PVC so it can be painted. Another thing that Sandpaper is good for is taking off layer lines off your 3D prints to give them that smooth finish that you see on 3D printed armor sets.
Heat Gun Heat guns are one of the most important tools that you could have. They're used for foam to heat seal it so it's not porous allowing it to be primed quicker and be painted with less paint. They're also used for heating the foam so it can be shaped into something it's not flat. Heat guns are also used when working with worbla making it shapeable as well.
Pins Pins can be used for more than just sewing. They can be used when tracing your patterns on to softer materials like foams. and they're useful when styling wigs, being used to hold the wig onto the wig head preventing movement.
Metalic Markers Many EVA foams are black, having a nice metalic marker is the idea for tracing patterns on to it. Silver is ideal.
For Electonics
Soldering Iron Soldering Iron is a must need if you are planning on putting lights to your costume. It's the tool you need to attach all the wires to the microchip and LED's
Colored LED Wires Colored Wires are also needed if you want to put Lights. The wires are the equipment that makes the connections between your microchip and LED lights. The colors are highly recommended so you can tell the difference between power, ground
Wire Strippers and Cutters Wire Strippers and cutters are needed to cut down the wires your using for LED lights and the strippers are needed to pull of the wax outer layer of the colored wires so when you solder the correct connections are made.
Wire Strippers and Cutters Wire Strippers and cutters are needed to cut down the wires your using for LED lights and the strippers are needed to pull of the wax outer layer of the colored wires so when you solder the correct connections are made.
Shrink Tubing Shirk tubing is nice when soldering it's something that can hold all your wires together making them more compact, therefore easier to hide inside your costume.
Adafruit Trinket Adafruit Trinket is the microchip that you should use to program your LEDs it's small and compact.
For Painting
Air brush Air brush is useful for large paints. When you paint with an Air brush it prevents visible brush marks giving your paint a more natural look.
Brushes Having paint brushes is straight forward, they used for detail painting, but also if you get some cheap ones or some acid brushes they used for spreading and applying things like primers and glues.
Craft/Sewing/Hair Scissors Different scissors do better at different things never use a pair of scissors for a task that they were intended for otherwise they will dull and become useless quickly
Miscellaneous
Hot Glue Gun For glueing...even wig hair
Wig Head If you are interested in any form of wig styling, you'll need a wig head. When buying a wig head get a 'male' wig head because they're closest to a normal sized human head.
Wig Head Clamp Wig head clamps are a necessity when it comes to wig styling wigs when it will hold your wig head in place making it possible to style it, otherwise, the Styrofoam will be too light and fall over.
Marterial Why
Foam and Thermal Plastic
EVA Foam EVA Foam is one of the most useful materials to have. It’s a major material for making any prop you would want to make and or any armor you want to make if you don’t have a 3d printer.
High Density Foam HD foam is a specilized EVA foam designed by SKS props. it was made specifically for crafting props and costumes.
LED Foam (Translucent Foam) LED Foam is essential if you want to add lights to your cosplay. It’s white and more pores than regular EVA foam making it ideal for covering LED lights.
Foam Clay Foam Clay is useful for smaller details. Since it’s a clay you can mold smaller details that you can’t achieve with carving EVA Foam.
Worbla Worbla is a thermal plastic, meaning you can shape it using heat. Worbla isn’t a material that you want to use excessively but it works great when you want to reinforce foam builds, or when you want to achieve a different textural look from foam.
Contact Cement Contact Cement is the glue to use when Working with EVA foam, I recommend barge contact cement.
Primer
Plasty Dip Plasty Dip is essentially a spray on rubber, it works great as a primer for EVA foam. Just don’t use it on LED foam otherwise it will take away the translucency that it has.
FlexBond FlexBond can be used as a primer, just make sure to water it down. It Brushes on white and dries clear. It can be used to prime Worbla and LED foam.
Autobody Filler and Primer Autobody filler and primer can be used for priming 3D print parts. It can also be used in the process of sanding off layer lines. Spray it on first and start sanding it down, spray it again, sand it down again. repeate untill smooth.
LEDs
LED Lights/Strips Can be used for lighting up props.
Wigs
Wig A good quality wig will help complete the look of a cosplay if the idea you’re trying to portray has unrealistic anime hair. Don’t use a cheap Halloween store wig. A good place to get a wig is Arda Wigs.
Miscellaneous
Cling Wrap Cling wrap or plastic wrap is useful when you want to make something tailored to your body. When you use it you’re going to wrap yourself in it cover it with tape and draw the pattern directly onto the tape.
PVC Pipe For poles on weapons or sword handles or any other place you need a pipe structure.
Card Stock For pattern making or for walls when silcone casting.

Inkscape

Inkscape is a free program that you can create vector files with. Vector files are compatible with Cricut machines which can be useful if you want to get an exact replica cut out of what you had made. You can also directly print out your vector file on paper and use that as a stencil for any prop that you are making

Fusion 360

Fusion 360 is a program that you can buy which you can make 3D models with that you can 3D print. It comes with a one year free trial.

Arduino

Arduino is a programming software that uses a c/c++ dialect. This is the program that you're going to use to program your microchip for any lights that are going to be incorporated into your costume

Tips for getting started!

Choose Your Character!

A good first step to take is to pick your favorite character you want to bring to life. He, she, it, they, can be from a video game, manga, anime, movie, comic or cartoon. Remember, it doesn't matter what age, race, or gender you are, nor does your body shape matter. Cosplay is for anyone, so just have fun doing it.

Find a Reference

There are numerous to make a costume, ranging anywhere from a beginner's skill level to advanced and covering a large variety of materials and techniques. If you don't know where to start, the internet is a great place to learn any skill or technique. Remember, a costume doesn't need to cost your life savings, a armor set can be made out of cardboard, or even a floor mat. Just by looking at what other people have done in the cosplay community is plenty to get a starting point.

Everyone has to Start Somewhere

As much as you may desire it, no one starts out a master-level Cosplayer, we started out wearing car mats as armor with store bought swords. Failing is a important process of cosplaying allowing for learning and growing. Just remember, don't be too hard on yourself, you're your own worst enemy if you dig yourself into a perfectionist hole. Use failing as a drive to get better and don't compare your skills with other peoples.
